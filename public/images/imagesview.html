Dolphins in Fujairah

Dolphins, famous for their intelligence and social behavior, have long captured the curiosity of scientists and nature lovers alike. Realizing the need to conduct comprehensive research on these captivating marine mammals, a project to study whales and dolphins has been underway in the Emirate of Fujairah since 2017. Which aims to acquire basic information, such as the existing species and behaviors of dolphins and whales, and to know their locations and protect them from ship traffic, fishing activities, and other threats.

The marine mammal study project at the Fujairah Research Center has achieved remarkable progress in the field of understanding and protecting marine ecosystems. Their extensive research efforts resulted in the identification of more than 15 species of dolphins and whales, including three new species that were recorded in the United Arab Emirates, namely the tropical spotted dolphin, The striped dolphin, and the rough-toothed dolphin. This pioneering work highlights the region's extraordinary biodiversity.

Biopsy samples were taken from them to evaluate their genetic diversity, and the results of this genetic study will provide important information about the adaptive capacity and preservation of the offshore dolphin population. In addition to identifying species and locations, the project focused on capturing acoustic data to gain insight into dolphin communication behavior. By recording the dolphins' vocalizations, the researchers gathered a valuable data set documenting their unique vocal signatures and communication patterns.

The groundbreaking discoveries of this project have far-reaching implications for marine conservation, potentially affecting habitat protection, species conservation efforts, and environmental management strategies.
